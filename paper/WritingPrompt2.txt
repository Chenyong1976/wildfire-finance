Review the revised manuscript as a skeptical journal referee and copy editor.

Identify passages that sound generic, repetitive, overstated, or disconnected from the
specific evidence in the paper. Pay particular attention to:
- broad claims that are not supported by a citation or result;
- paragraphs with predictable or formulaic structure;
- repeated summaries of the same finding;
- excessive use of abstract nouns;
- sentences that obscure the economic mechanism;
- transitions that do not express a real logical connection;
- causal language that is stronger than the research design supports.

For each issue, show:
1. The original sentence or passage.
2. Why it is weak.
3. A more precise replacement.
4. Whether the replacement requires author verification.

Do not make stylistic changes merely for the sake of sounding different. Preserve the
author’s voice and the manuscript’s substantive content.
